about us Bill Raiford

Hi, I'm Bill Raiford. I love all living things; each a single thread woven into the one tapestry of life; each a single melody in the one universal symphony; each a single cell in the one body that overcomes entropy.

I don't necessarily like all living things.

I conceived ibgib well over twenty years ago when I first moved from information based on knowledge to that based on belief. Although perhaps the first remembrance of this I have is as an 8th grade math nerd, I realized that if I ever were to make an "error" that I then had a non-zero probability of any statement I made to contain that error. This was seemingly quite inescapable!

And I could not - and still cannot - read philosophy.

So I wrote, and continue to write, code instead, focused on this belief-based approach, which over the years has gone through several iterations, the most recent prototype of which you are currently sampling. As it turns out, instilling humility and belief into a code base at its foundations ends up looking extremely biological.

So if you're interested in the intersection of non-Shannon, belief-based computer science and biological coding; where data and metadata sit alongside each other; where theorems are more theorem-y and less proof-y; and where we can perhaps gain insight into other seemingly disparate topics such as money, knowledge, life & death...to you I say welcome!

Let us weave our threads, harmonize our melodies, and enjoin our bodies as we strive to overcome that entropic night.
